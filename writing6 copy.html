A Sly Pompous Grin

By Madison Smith

What is it called to do a thing seldom done?

What is the name for the moment sun shines upon a stone
And is decided to be sat up on?

In a shallow creek
Life is seen flourishing and fluorescent.

A fish leaps.
An insect trills.
And all sources and sounds become quite incandescent.

What do you call it then
When sun’s light is allowed to be shown on one’s face?

In spite of all humanity’s judgements
And forgotten grace.

These moments twist and they writhe
They settle then remain still

They dissolve that which would have only one or the other
By way of their force and their will

Is there a word that can even approach capturing these moments?
A thought which may contain it all?

To utter such a thing
Surely can only bring about their death
And their fall

For such moments really are not meant to be captured
Nor contained at all

This would solve their mysteries
And unravel the riddle of the summer giving way to the fall

The death brought by winter
The rebirth from its thaw
The sprouting green shoots pushing up through it
Stretching
And tall

It is for this reason which mystery remains

Wisdom, only wisdom comes close to that which waxes
and wanes

Patience comes close also
As it glimmers like flames

Surrender illuminates a truth of oneness when no One
Remains

These moments shatter all boundaries
They requestion all answers
Solving such moments, it confuses,
It sequesters
It is nothing short of hubris
A sly pompous grin

Seek these moments out
Yet, do not name them

And feel the name revealed
Within.
